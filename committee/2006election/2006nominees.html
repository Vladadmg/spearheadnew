Announcing the Nominees for Super Committee 2006

Katie Heibein

This past season I was fortunate to have had two super roles: one in Dr. Atomic as a WAC and the other as the slithering Salamander in Flute for Kids. In the past 3-plus years I have also met many of you doing lightwalking assignments and working at the Gay Pride events. I have a lot of energy and would love to have the opportunity to put it to good use serving on the Super Committee. As a member, I would continue to build on the work that has been done by the existing committee, which would include working with the opera staff to improve opportunities for more super roles, expanded bake sales and once again another GREAT Party. Your consideration and support is appreciated.

Andrew Korniej
Long-term Super (1989 to present) with 30 productions to my credit, award-winning Spearhead columnist, featured artiste in SFO 2004 La Boheme (highlights televised on 60 Minutes) and Super Committee Member from 2001 – 2004.
After a two year hiatus I would love to get back onto the Super Committee and be a part of the new developments coming to San Francisco Opera. We are a unique and remarkable group of individuals and I have always been proud to be a part of that group and to be of service in helping make the supering experience enjoyable and memorable.

Jim Miller
 I have been a Super since the 1989 fall season when I appeared in Aida. Since that time, I have supered in 17 operas. I have greatly enjoyed this experience and would like to help pass along this special tradition to others. Many of you may know me as the backstage photographer who took your picture in costume and gave you a print. Twice before I have served on the Super Committee, so I know what the demands of this position are. I would like to add my knowledge, experience and expertise to the mix for the upcoming season. With the appointment of David Gockley as the new General Director, we have an excellent opportunity to bring supering back to its “glory days.” It would be my pleasure to work for the Supers and to help plan and produce a really good season-closer party. I look forward to seeing all of you in the 2006 – 2007 season!

Barbara Sherman
 I have been on the Super Committee for one year and really enjoy it. I was the co-chairman of the 2005 Super Party with Paul and it was a "Super" success. I have been active with the S.F. Opera for 8 years and have been a Super three times and a continuous Lightwalker. I am a Matinee Monitor, a docent with Opera aLa Carte at the schools, and a follower giving tours of the Opera House. And as an aside, I founded the Harrisburg Opera Association in Pennsylvania before I moved to California. I would love to continue to serve on the Super Committee to help advance its goals.

Paul Szczesiul

I would like to have the opportunity for my third and final year as a member of the Super Committee. As a member for the past two years I have been fortunate to work with an outstanding group of talented people to serve our Super community. During this past year, the committee has accomplished a great deal including--
--Planning and executing all the fundraising events
--Organizing a terrific Annual Party
--Conducting a survey of all Supers (results to be published this spring)
--Maintaining good communication with the San Francisco Opera company during the management transition
--Increasing the communication with Supers
--Rewriting the Super Manual (coming this fall)
And there is still much to do. I hope you give me the opportunity to continue this important work.

Kimberly Thompson

 

I have always worked to promote the Supers in roles on stage and at events and for us to have a higher quality profile within the House.

 

Walt Thorp

I truly love all that the San Francisco Opera represents: music, art, and drama. The Supers that support that effort deserve and have earned the respect of the company. I would work to foster improved conditions:
1. Work-out space/gym space to use during "down times"
2. Healthier vending machine options
3. Some limited access (per production) to film archives so Supers could view themselves, as never before, on stage.

Laurel Winzler

I’ve been a Super at SFO since 1983 (appearing in more than 30 productions), and you can read all about my past supering experiences in the interviews section of this website. I served on the Super Committee from 1989 – 1991, and I’d like to get involved again, as we transition into the new administration, to deal with issues that affect Supers and to take positive steps to improve the supering experience for us all. I’m concerned about the need for reliable rehearsal schedules, the question of the increased expense and scarcity of safe parking near the Opera House, and overall personal security (especially for Supers who are called or released at off times).  With 20 years of perspective on what has and hasn’t worked in the past, I’d like to participate in bringing the experience of all Supers to a new level of professionalism and fun. 

Rob Wonder

As a current member of the Super Committee, I very much would like to serve one final year on your behalf. This would allow me the opportunity to work with the others to help in our efforts to implement the results of the Super Survey.

Based on your feedback, we will work even harder to keep all Supers informed and will work with the House and among ourselves on finding ways to create a more Super-friendly atmosphere. It has been my honor to serve you these past two years.

Please print the Ballot, make your selections, and mail so that it is received by May 26, 2006. The results will be announced after the June Committee meeting.
